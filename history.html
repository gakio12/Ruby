History

February 24, 1993 – Ruby is conceived by Yukihiro Matsumoto, in an online conversation with colleague Keiju Ishitsuka. While Ruby is named for a colleague’s birthstone, inspired by the use of a gemstone for Perl, he later realized the following comparisons: In birthstones, pearl is June, ruby is July. When measuring font sizes, pearl is 5pt, ruby is 5.5pt[1]. Ruby seemed a natural progression from Perl. In stating his inspiration for the language, “I wanted a scripting language that was more powerful than Perl, and more object-oriented than Python[2].”
